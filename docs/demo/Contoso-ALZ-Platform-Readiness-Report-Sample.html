§ 6 — Critical Issues & Course of Action

Absence of Centralized Network Security Controls

Design Area: Network Topology and Connectivity

Severity: High

No Azure Firewall or hub-spoke topology detected. Without centralized network security controls, all workloads are exposed to lateral movement risks, uncontrolled egress, and inconsistent perimeter enforcement across subscriptions.

Course of Action: Deploy a hub VNet with Azure Firewall to centralize egress and east-west traffic filtering. Establish hub-spoke peering for all workload spokes. Enable DDoS Protection Standard on VNets with public-facing resources.

DDoS Protection Not Enabled

Design Area: Network Topology and Connectivity

Severity: High

Virtual networks with public IP addresses lack DDoS Protection Standard. This exposes internet-facing workloads to volumetric, protocol, and application-layer DDoS attacks that could cause service disruption.

Course of Action: Enable Azure DDoS Network Protection on all VNets containing public IP resources. Configure DDoS diagnostic logging to the centralized Log Analytics workspace for attack visibility.

Management Group Hierarchy Not ALZ-Aligned

Design Area: Governance

Severity: Medium

No Platform, Connectivity, or Landing Zones management groups detected. Without the standard ALZ hierarchy, policy inheritance is fragmented, RBAC boundaries are unclear, and subscription governance cannot scale consistently.

Course of Action: Implement the Azure Landing Zone management group hierarchy: Root → Platform (Identity, Management, Connectivity) + Landing Zones (Corp, Online). Assign foundational policy initiatives at the appropriate management group scope.

Defender Security Posture Gaps

Design Area: Security

Severity: High

Defender for Cloud assessment health is critically low with multiple unhealthy findings. Secure Score is not measurable. This prevents effective risk visibility and automated threat detection across the platform.

Course of Action: Enable all critical Defender for Cloud plans (CSPM, Servers, Storage, Key Vault). Remediate high-impact security recommendations. Configure continuous export of security alerts to the centralized workspace.

Insufficient Monitoring and Diagnostics Coverage

Design Area: Management

Severity: High

Diagnostics coverage is below 10% and no centralized Log Analytics workspace topology is established. Sentinel is not deployed. This blocks incident detection, compliance auditing, and operational troubleshooting at scale.

Course of Action: Deploy a centralized Log Analytics workspace with 90+ day retention. Configure Azure Policy to enforce diagnostic settings on all resource types. Deploy Microsoft Sentinel for security event correlation and automated response.
